Catalyst Materials Design and Construction

Design and construction of novel catalytic materials, including nano-structured catalysts, single-atom catalysts, and molecular catalysts for energy conversion and environmental remediation. Through precise control of the electronic structure and coordination environment of catalytic active sites, efficient catalytic reactions are achieved.

  • Design and preparation of single-atom catalysts
  • Catalytic applications of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs)
  • Design and characterization of bifunctional catalysts
  • Development of in-situ characterization techniques

Photocatalytic Hydrogen Production

Development of photocatalytic materials and systems that directly utilize sunlight to split water for hydrogen production, including semiconductor photocatalysts and photoelectrochemical electrodes, to achieve efficient and low-cost renewable hydrogen production technologies.

  • Highly efficient visible-light-responsive photocatalytic materials
  • Z-scheme photocatalytic water splitting systems
  • Integration and scaling of photoelectrochemical devices
  • Construction of artificial photosynthetic systems

CO2 Resource Utilization

Development of CO2 catalytic conversion technologies to transform CO2 into high-value chemicals and fuels through various conversion pathways including photocatalysis, electrocatalysis, and thermocatalysis, contributing to carbon neutrality goals.

  • Electrocatalytic reduction of CO2 to C1-C3 chemicals
  • Photocatalytic conversion of CO2 to methanol/methane
  • CO2 hydrogenation for high-value products
  • Perovskite solar cell technology

In-situ Characterization and Reaction Mechanism Studies

Development of advanced in-situ/operando characterization techniques to reveal the structural evolution of active centers and the formation of reaction intermediates during catalytic processes, providing scientific basis for in-depth understanding of catalytic mechanisms and targeted catalyst design.

  • In-situ infrared/Raman spectroscopy characterization
  • X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) studies
  • Environmental transmission electron microscopy (ETEM)
  • Transient spectroscopy and kinetic analysis
