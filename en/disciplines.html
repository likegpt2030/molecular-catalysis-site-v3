Molecular Catalysis and In-situ Characterization Research Group

Our research group is dedicated to the development and application of molecular catalysis and in-situ characterization techniques. Through multidisciplinary research, we focus on exploring catalytic reaction mechanisms and developing efficient catalytic materials to address major challenges in energy conversion, environmental remediation, and chemical synthesis.

Photoelectrochemistry: Photo- and Electrocatalysis

This direction focuses on the study of photocatalytic, electrocatalytic, and thermocatalytic processes, developing efficient energy conversion catalysts and systems. Our research team is dedicated to designing novel catalytic materials for efficient conversion and utilization of renewable energy.

  • Photocatalytic water splitting for hydrogen production
  • Electrocatalytic CO2 utilization technologies and catalyst development
  • Thermocatalytic reaction mechanisms and catalyst optimization
  • Design of novel multifunctional composite catalysts

Artificial Photosynthesis

This research direction aims to mimic natural photosynthesis processes, achieving the conversion of water, carbon dioxide, and nitrogen into high-value chemicals and clean energy sources like hydrogen.

  • H2O+CO2+N2→ Chemicals+O2
  • H2O → H2+ O2
  • Design and integration of artificial photosynthesis systems
  • Efficient water splitting and CO₂ conversion systems

Condensed Matter Photoelectric Physics and Chemistry

This direction studies photoelectric phenomena in condensed matter and their chemical foundations, exploring novel semiconductor materials, semiconductor-metal interfaces, and material design methods to provide theoretical support for harnessing short-wavelength photon conversion.

  • Research on novel perovskites and organic-inorganic materials
  • Semiconductor-electrolyte interface dynamics research
  • Exploration of photoelectric electron process mechanisms
  • Efficient charge separation and transfer control

Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence

Integration of machine learning and artificial intelligence technologies with catalytic science research, developing data-driven material design strategies and high-throughput experimental methods to accelerate the discovery and optimization of novel catalysts.

  • Deep learning-driven materials design
  • Machine learning models for reaction mechanism studies
  • Autonomous experimental robots and high-throughput screening
  • Catalytic system design combining quantum computing and AI

Synthetic Biology and Biomanufacturing

Combining biomanufacturing and chemical catalysis technologies to develop enzyme-based catalytic systems and artificial photosynthesis for downstream biochemical production, exploring the integration of microbial communities with inorganic catalytic systems.

  • Integrated systems of enzymatic and inorganic catalysis
  • Light-driven biochemical transformations
  • Integration of artificial photosynthesis with biological systems
  • Bio-based sustainable food production methods
